Hospital Associated Infections

Summary: Analysis and Findings

The purpose of this project was to seek any correlation between hospital size, source of funding, and type of facility to the incidence of Hospital Associated Infections. Using HAI data from data.gov and hospital demographic data of approximately 4000 hospitals, each infection measure was analyzed against each hospital demographic.

Data cleaning was exhaustive and comprehensive, skewing the results by hospital type, due to absence of data reported by the hospitals.

We used matplotlib to graph each of the infection types against the number of beds per facility. However, due to underreporting per hospital type, no valid conclusion could be made.

This scatter plot represents the variety of infections across all hospital types, based on the number of beds in each hospital. The dot size is proportional to the percentage of each infection.

This visualization shows that the incidence of Clostridium difficile infection is not dependent on hospital size, ocurring in greater cases for larger hospitals, but with a smaller percentage due to more beds.

CDI is common in long hospital stays due to antibiotic use and overuse and it's transmissibility. Based on the data available, it is unclear as to what role infection control procedures affect the transmission. The data also lacks clinical correlation of CDI cases due to antibiotic use, as this is difficult to determine, thefore not reported.
